## DMCA Notification Procedure

If you believe that your copyrighted work is being infringed and is accessible through NeXtv, we will address your concerns, but please understand our limitations.

### Before Filing a DMCA Notice

**IMPORTANT**: Please determine the actual source of the infringing content:

1. **Is the content hosted by NeXtv?** NO - We do not host any content
2. **Is the content provided by a third-party IPTV service?** YES - Contact that service provider directly
3. **Does NeXtv have the ability to remove the content?** NO - We cannot remove content we don't host

### When to Contact NeXtv

Contact us if:
- You believe NeXtv's software itself (not third-party content) infringes your copyright
- You have evidence that NeXtv is actively promoting or facilitating access to specific infringing content
- You believe user-generated content within our app (reviews, comments, avatars) infringes your copyright
- A third-party IPTV provider is unresponsive and you need assistance identifying them

### When to Contact the IPTV Provider

Contact the IPTV provider directly if:
- Copyrighted content is being streamed through their service
- They are hosting, transmitting, or distributing infringing material
- You want the content removed from their servers

## Filing a DMCA Takedown Notice

If, after reading the above, you still believe NeXtv is the appropriate party to contact, you may submit a DMCA takedown notice to our designated Copyright Agent.

### Required Information

Your DMCA notice must include ALL of the following information, as required by 17 U.S.C. ยง 512(c)(3):

1. **Identification of the copyrighted work** claimed to have been infringed:
   - Specific title(s) and description(s) of the copyrighted work
   - If multiple works, a representative list
   - Registration number(s) if registered with the U.S. Copyright Office

2. **Identification of the infringing material** and information sufficient to locate it:
   - Specific URLs or locations within the NeXtv app
   - Screenshots or detailed descriptions
   - Date and time when infringement was observed
   - Steps to reproduce the alleged infringement

3. **Your contact information**:
   - Full legal name
   - Physical mailing address
   - Telephone number
   - Email address

4. **A statement of good faith belief**:
   - "I have a good faith belief that use of the copyrighted material described above is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law."

5. **A statement of accuracy under penalty of perjury**:
   - "I swear, under penalty of perjury, that the information in this notification is accurate and that I am the copyright owner or am authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed."

6. **Physical or electronic signature**:
   - Handwritten signature on a printed document, or
   - Electronic signature (typed name, digital signature, etc.)

## Our Response Process

Upon receiving a valid DMCA takedown notice, we will:

### Step 1: Review (1-2 business days)
- Verify the notice contains all required information
- Assess whether the allegedly infringing material is within our control
- Determine if we are the appropriate party to address the claim

### Step 2: Action (3-5 business days)
- **If the content is within our control** (e.g., user-generated content, app screenshots): Remove or disable access promptly
- **If the content is hosted by a third party**: Inform you that we cannot remove content we don't host and provide guidance on contacting the actual content provider
- Notify the alleged infringer (if applicable)

### Step 3: Notification
- Send confirmation to the complainant
- Provide counter-notification opportunity to the user (if applicable)
- Document the claim and our actions

### Special Circumstances

**Third-Party IPTV Content**: If the alleged infringement relates to content hosted by a third-party IPTV provider:
- We will inform you that we cannot remove the content
- We may provide information to help you identify the IPTV provider (if available and legally permissible)
- We may investigate if there is evidence of direct facilitation of infringement

## Counter-Notification Procedure

If you believe that content you posted was removed or disabled by mistake or misidentification, you may file a DMCA counter-notification.

### Required Information for Counter-Notice

Your counter-notification must include:

1. **Your contact information**:
   - Full legal name
   - Physical address
   - Telephone number
   - Email address

2. **Identification of the removed material**:
   - Description of the material that was removed
   - Location where it appeared before removal

3. **Statement under penalty of perjury**:
   - "I swear, under penalty of perjury, that I have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification."

4. **Consent to jurisdiction**:
   - "I consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for the judicial district in which my address is located (or [specify district] if outside the United States), and I will accept service of process from the person who provided the DMCA notification or an agent of such person."

5. **Physical or electronic signature**

### Counter-Notice Process

1. **Submit your counter-notice** to: dmca@nextv.app
2. **We will forward your counter-notice** to the original complainant
3. **10-14 business day waiting period**: The complainant has 10-14 business days to file a court action
4. **Restoration**: If no legal action is filed, we may restore the content within 10-14 business days

## Repeat Infringer Policy

### Three-Strike Policy

In accordance with the DMCA and our Terms of Service, NeXtv has adopted a policy of terminating, in appropriate circumstances, users who are deemed to be repeat infringers.

**First Offense**: Warning and removal of infringing content  
**Second Offense**: Temporary suspension of account (7-30 days)  
**Third Offense**: Permanent termination of account

### What Constitutes Repeat Infringement

Users may be considered repeat infringers if:
- They receive multiple valid DMCA takedown notices for different works
- They repeatedly restore removed content
- They circumvent our systems to access previously blocked content
- They knowingly and repeatedly violate copyright law

### Appeals Process

Users who believe they have been wrongly identified as repeat infringers may appeal by contacting legal@nextv.app with evidence of:
- Counter-notifications that were successful
- Proof of license or authorization
- Evidence of mistake or misidentification

## Misrepresentation and False Claims

### Penalties Under the DMCA

**WARNING**: Under Section 512(f) of the DMCA, any person who knowingly materially misrepresents that:
- Material is infringing, or
- Material was removed or disabled by mistake or misidentification

**May be subject to liability for damages**, including costs and attorneys' fees.

### What Constitutes Misrepresentation

False claims include:
- Claiming copyright ownership you don't possess
- Identifying non-infringing material as infringing
- Filing claims for competitive or malicious purposes
- Knowingly providing false information in a DMCA notice

### Our Actions Against False Claims

We reserve the right to:
- Report false claimants to appropriate authorities
- Seek damages for processing false claims
- Share information about false claimants with other service providers
- Ban users who file false DMCA claims
